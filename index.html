What is the project about?

  • Diffusion language models hold the promise of fast parallel generation, while autoregressive (AR) models typically excel in quality. But why choose? TiDAR allows you have the best of both worlds in one model with zero overhead by leveraging the unused compute to memory density on GPUs.
  • We introduce TiDAR, a sequence-level hybrid architecture that drafts tokens (Thinking) in Diffusion and samples final outputs (Talking) in AutoRegression - all within a single forward pass using a structured attention mask.
  • We extensively evaluate TiDAR against AR models, speculative decoding, and diffusion variants across generative and likelihood tasks at 1.5B and 8B scales. Thanks to the parallel drafting and sampling as well as exact KV cache support, TiDAR outperforms speculative decoding in measured throughput and surpasses diffusion models like Dream and Llada in both efficiency and quality. Most notably, TiDAR is the first architecture to close the quality gap with AR models while delivering 4.71x to 5.91x more tokens per second.

The Secret Sauce: Free Token Slots

The power of modern GPUs gets fully utilized under a balanced load between compute density and memory IO. Increasing batch size can increase the compute density by weights reusing but this requires loading more KV cache for each sample and more aggressive model splitting. We can actually pass in more tokens to the forward with similar latency. Under the context of TiDAR, we pass several mask tokens to perform diffusion drafting and several pre-drafted tokens to perform autoregressive sampling. These extra tokens can be computed with almost the same latency, and therefore, we refer to these as "free token slots".

Sequence-level Hybrid Attention


We conduct parallel diffusion drafting and autoregressive sampling in a single forward pass with a structured attention mask. So the prefix tokens and those that are waiting to be verified are attended causally while the thinking diffusion tokens are attended in a bidirectional manner.

Diffusion Self-Speculation with Autoregressive Verification

With the help of the custom mask, we can verify the proposals from last step as well as pre-draft new proposals starting at every single prefix in a single forward. Plus, we guarantee one token per forward pass, similar to speculative decoding but with more added advantages. Given the right draft length, we can maximally leverage the free token slots and achieve the best of both worlds. See top demo for illustration.

Why is TiDAR special?

  • TiDAR achieves SOTA inference latency under small batch sizes (better than speculative decoding such as EAGLE-v3).
  • TiDAR outperforms the quality-efficiency trade-offs than open source DLLMs such as Dream and Llada.
  • TiDAR is a standalone model that is easy to train and deploy with minimal hyperparameters to tune and low serving overhead.
  • TiDAR has extremely rich training signals, even better than AR models, thanks to its dual objective.

Main Results

Quality-Efficiency Trade-offs

TiDAR achieves impressive speedup over traditional AR models, and stays impressively competitive to SOTA speculative decoding method, EAGLE-v3, giving a wall-clock token per second throughput boost from 4.71x to 5.91x with PyTorch + Flex Attention implementation.

Likelihood & Generative Performance


We compare TiDAR with AR models (Llama, SmolLM, Qwen) and diffusion models (Dream, Llada, Block Diffusion) on downstream tasks. With only continual pretraining, not only is TiDAR outperforming diffusion models (with the best quality at 1T/NFE) but also matches or significantly close the gap to AR models.

Ablation Studies

In our paper, we also conducted detailed ablation studies to justify our design choices:
  • Pareto Frontier under the same training recipe.
  • Comparing TiDAR's parallel decoding with other popular diffusion denoising strategies (such as threshold-based and left-to-right).
  • TiDAR gives the flexibility to verify with AR and diffusion predictions, we showcase their effects on downstream performance.
  • One of the critical advantages of TiDAR is that we use one-step diffusion for efficient drafting. To further boost its performance, we apply a full masking strategy to bridge train-test consistency and improve loss signal density.
